WELCOME TO BARAUNI REFINERY

Barauni Refinery a state owned oil Refinery of IndianOil was dedicated to the nation in1965. Established in 1964, on the Northern banks of holy Ganga, at Barauni, on the periphery of the district town Begusarai, Bihar. Situated 125 km from Patna, it is also the point where two important railways meet – the Eastern Railways and North Eastern Railways. It was built in collaboration with the Soviet Union with limited participation from Romania, at a cost of ~Rs 49.4 Crore and went on stream in 1964.

The Refinery has gone a long way over the years, starting from a crude processing capacity of 1.0 MMTPA (AVU-I) of sweet crude from Assam oil fields. It has steadily expanded its capacity to 3.0 MMTPA with AVU-II and AU-III. Further, with the revamp of AVU-I & II in 1990 and AU-III in 2000, crude processing capability was increased to 4.2 MMTPA till 2000 and then to current level of 6.0 MMTPA in 2002 enabling the processing of high sulphur crudes through Barauni Expansion Project (BXP). Residue Fluidized Catalytic Cracker Unit (RFCCU), Diesel Hydro Treating Unit (DHDT), Sulphur Recovery Unit (SRU), Amine Re-generation Unit (ARU) and Hydrogen Generation Unit (HGU) are some of the major units.

Different Departments

REFINERY DIVISION

The refinery division of an oil refinery processes crude oil into valuable petroleum products through the following steps:
Crude Oil Reception: Crude oil is received and stored, undergoing preliminary testing.
Pre-Treatment: Impurities like water, sediments, and salts are removed from the crude oil.
Distillation: The crude oil is heated to separate it into different fractions based on boiling points.
Conversion Processes: Certain fractions undergo additional processes to modify their molecular structure and improve product quality.
Treatment and Blending: Impurities are removed, and different fractions are blended to meet product specifications.
Product Distribution: Refined petroleum products are stored and transported to various markets.
Throughout the process, quality control measures and environmental regulations are followed to ensure high-quality products and minimize environmental impact.

PIPELINE DIVISION

Oil refinery pipelines transport crude oil and refined products within the refinery complex and to storage terminals.
They are made of durable steel and use pump stations, valves, and control systems to regulate flow.
Pipelines are segregated by product type and undergo regular inspection and maintenance for safety.
Their efficient operation is crucial for maintaining the supply chain and optimizing production in oil refineries.

MARKETING DIVISION

The marketing division of an oil refinery is responsible for the distribution, promotion,
and sale of petroleum products to customers and end-users.
This division plays a crucialrole in ensuring the efficient and
profitable marketing of the refinery's products in the marketplace.

Efficient product distribution: The marketing division oversees the efficient distribution of petroleum products.
Customer relationship management: Building strong customer relationships is a priority.
Brand promotion: The marketing division focuses on promoting the refinery's brand.
Pricing strategies: Implementing effective pricing strategies.
Market analysis: Conducting thorough market research and analysis.
Regulatory compliance: Ensuring compliance with regulations.
Market expansion: Identifying new market opportunities and strategies.

LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT

The Learning and Development division in an oil refinery plays a crucial role in training and developing interns,
providing them with valuable skills and knowledge for their professional growth. The division focuses on creating
a comprehensive and structured training program to ensure that interns receive a well-rounded learning
experience during their time at the refinery. Orientation: Interns receive a comprehensive introduction to the refinery's operations, safety protocols, and organizational structure. Technical Training: Interns gain hands-on experience in operating refinery equipment and learn about process flow and unit functions.
Mentorship and Guidance: Assigned mentors provide support, advice, and help interns navigate the refinery's work environment. Cross-Functional Exposure: Interns work in different departments to develop a broader understanding of refinery operations.
Classroom Sessions and Workshops: Supplemental sessions cover safety regulations, industry best practices, and relevant technical skills. Project Assignments: Interns tackle specific projects to apply their skills and develop problem-solving abilities and teamwork.
Performance Evaluation and Feedback: Regular evaluations and feedback sessions aid interns' professional development. Networking Opportunities: Interns have chances to connect with refinery professionals through events and gatherings.
